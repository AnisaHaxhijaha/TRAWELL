Explore Istanbul: Activities and Attractions

Topkapi Palace
Topkapi Palace stands as a majestic symbol of Ottoman power and grandeur in Istanbul, showcasing exquisite architecture, lush courtyards, and a treasury brimming with priceless artifacts and jewels.
Bosphorus
The Bosphorus, a narrow strait in Turkey, serves as the natural boundary between Europe and Asia, connecting the Black Sea to the Sea of Marmara.
Dolmabahce Palace
Dolmabahçe Palace, an architectural gem on the Bosphorus, was the Ottoman Empire's administrative hub from 1856 to 1922, boasting opulent designs blending Baroque, Rococo, and Neoclassical styles.
Galata Tower
Galata, a historic neighborhood in Istanbul, is renowned for the iconic Galata Tower, which offers panoramic views of the city and the Bosphorus. The area is a vibrant blend of old and new, featuring bustling streets lined with cafes, shops, and art galleries, reflecting its rich cultural heritage.
Hagia Sophia
Hagia Sophia, originally built as a cathedral in Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul) in 537 AD, is renowned for its massive dome and impressive Byzantine architecture. Over the centuries, it has served as a mosque, a museum, and recently was reconverted back into a mosque in 2020.
Istanbul Aquarium
Istanbul Aquarium, one of the largest thematic aquariums in the world, offers visitors an immersive experience with over 17,000 sea and land creatures across its extensive exhibits.
Sultan Ahmed
The Sultan Ahmed Mosque, commonly known as the Blue Mosque, is an iconic historical mosque in Istanbul, Turkey. It is renowned for its striking blue İznik tile interior, six minarets, and a grand central dome, making it one of the most famous and visited landmarks in Istanbul.
